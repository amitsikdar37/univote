Frequently Asked Questions

What is UNI-VOTE?
UNI-VOTE is a secure, next-generation voting platform that uses blockchain technology and Zero-Knowledge Proofs. It allows anyone to host and participate in elections where every vote is completely private and the final results are fully verifiable and tamper-proof.
Is my vote really anonymous? How?
Yes, your vote is 100% anonymous. When you cast your ballot, our Zero-Knowledge Proof (ZKP) system generates a cryptographic proof that confirms you are an eligible voter without revealing who you are. Your vote is mathematically separated from your identity before it's ever recorded on the blockchain. No one, not even us, can link you to your specific vote.
If it's anonymous, how can I be sure the results are fair?
This is the power of blockchain. While your identity is private, the vote itself is recorded on a public, unchangeable ledger. This creates a transparent audit trail where anyone can verify that all valid votes were counted correctly and that no votes were added or removed. It’s the perfect balance of individual privacy and public verifiability.
Why do I need to connect my accounts, like Google or X?
Connecting accounts is used to verify your eligibility for specific elections that have custom rules set by the host (e.g., "Only for members with an @iitp.ac.in email"). Our system only checks that you meet the criteria; your personal data is never linked to your final vote. This allows for powerful, flexible, and secure community voting.
What is a "gas fee" and do I need to pay to vote?
A gas fee is a very small transaction cost required by the blockchain network to record a transaction. We've built UNI-VOTE on the Base blockchain, where these fees are typically extremely low (often less than a cent). While this small network fee is part of the process, we are actively exploring ways to sponsor these fees for users in the future to make voting completely free.
How does UNI-VOTE's technology actually work?
At its core, UNI-VOTE uses an "Attested Claim-Nonce" model. First, our secure backend (acting as an Oracle) verifies your eligibility for an election based on the host's rules. If you are eligible, it issues you a unique, single-use "digital ticket" (a nonce). You then use this ticket, along with a personal secret only you know, to generate a Zero-Knowledge Proof. This proof confirms to the blockchain that you hold a valid ticket, without revealing which ticket belongs to you, ensuring your vote is secure and anonymous.
What is the voting process in simple steps?
Voting on UNI-VOTE is designed to be simple: 1. Register: For each election, you first register to have your eligibility checked. If successful, you receive your unique "digital ticket" for that vote. 2. Vote: Choose your preferred option and click the "Vote" button. Our system generates a secure ZK proof for you in the background. 3. Confirm: A transaction will pop up in your wallet. Simply approve it to cast your vote securely and privately onto the blockchain.
